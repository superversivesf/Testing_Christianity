# Episode 1 Fact-Check — Testing Christianity Part 1: Introduction / The Test

**Scope:** Verifiable factual claims only. Theological/interpretive claims (covenant theology, eschatology, identity of Israel, the validity of "Yahuwah" vs. other names) are outside scope, as they belong to ongoing scholarly and religious debate.

---

## Claim 1: "Christianity is the largest religion in the world"

**Verdict: TRUE**

**Finding:** Christianity is consistently ranked as the world's largest religion by population. According to the Pew Research Center's 2010 Global Religious Landscape study (updated in 2020 analyses), Christians made up approximately 2.3 billion of the then ~8 billion global population (about 29%). The Center for the Study of Global Christianity at Gordon-Conwell Theological Seminary has placed the number above 2.5 billion in recent estimates. No other single religion surpasses Christianity in total adherents.

**Sources:**
- [Pew Research Center — Global Religious Landscape](https://www.pewresearch.org/religion/2012/12/18/global-religious-landscape-exec/)
- [World Christianity Encyclopedia / Center for the Study of Global Christianity](https://worldchristian database.org/)
- [Wikipedia — Christianity by country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christianity_by_country)

---

## Claim 2: "Almost one-third of the world identify as Christians" / "2.5 billion people classify themselves as Christians"

**Verdict: MIXED (mostly accurate with caveats)**

**Finding:** The "one-third" figure is broadly correct as a rough approximation. The 2.5 billion figure is at the higher end of widely accepted estimates. Pew's 2010 data put Christians at ~2.17 billion (31%); more recent estimates from the Center for the Study of Global Christianity placed the number around 2.5 billion by ~2020–2022. The exact figure depends on whether one counts cultural/ancestral identification only, active believers, or formal membership. The speaker is not materially wrong, but precise numbers vary by source and methodology.

**Sources:**
- [Pew Research Center — Global Religious Landscape (2010)](https://www.pewresearch.org/religion/2012/12/18/global-religious-landscape-exec/)
- [Worldometers — World Population by Religion](https://www.worldometers.info/world-population/)
- [Center for the Study of Global Christianity — Status of Global Christianity](https://www.gordonconwell.edu/wp-content/uploads/2024/01/Status-of-Global-Christianity-2024.pdf)

## Claim 3: "More than half of [Christians] identify as Catholics" / "of the almost 2.5 billion people that identify as Christians, more than half of them are Catholics"

**Verdict: TRUE (approximately)**

**Finding:** The Catholic Church reports approximately 1.3–1.4 billion baptized members. If total Christians are estimated at 2.3–2.5 billion, Catholics constitute roughly 50–55%. Pew's 2010 data showed Catholics at roughly 50% of all Christians (with Protestants at 37%, Orthodox at 12%, and others at 1%). The speaker's statement is substantially correct.

**Sources:**
- [Pew Research Center — Catholic Church membership data](https://www.pewresearch.org/religion/)
- [Catholic Church statistics — Annuario Pontificio](https://www.catholic-hierarchy.org/country/sc1.html)
- [Wikipedia — Catholic Church](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catholic_Church)

## Claim 4: "There are over 8 billion people in the world"

**Verdict: TRUE**

**Finding:** The United Nations Population Division and Worldometer both place the global human population above 8 billion as of 2022–2023, with 2025–2026 estimates around 8.2–8.3 billion.

**Sources:**
- [UN World Population Prospects 2024](https://population.un.org/wpp/)
- [Worldometer — Current World Population](https://www.worldometers.info/world-population/)

## Claim 5: "The United States was once classified... as a Christian country" / "They were surrounded around Christianity for decades, centuries maybe... they claimed to be Christians in this country from the get-go"

**Verdict: MIXED**

**Finding:** The United States has never been constitutionally or legally established as a Christian nation. The First Amendment explicitly prohibits the establishment of a state religion, and the 1797 Treaty of Tripoli (ratified unanimously by the Senate) states: "the Government of the United States of America is not, in any sense, founded on the Christian religion." However, it is historically accurate that American culture, demographics, and political rhetoric have long been dominated by Christianity. Several colonies were founded with explicit Christian religious purposes, and the U.S. was long described by observers and leaders as a culturally Christian or "Protestant" nation. The speaker's phrasing "classified as" is imprecise, but the underlying observation about cultural dominance is accurate.

**Sources:**
- [Treaty of Tripoli, Article 11 (1797)](https://avalon.law.yale.edu/18th_century/bar1796t.asp)
- [Religion in the United States — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Religion_in_the_United_States)
- [First Amendment to the United States Constitution](https://www.archives.gov/founding-docs/bill-of-rights-transcript)

## Claim 7: "There are more churches in this world than hospitals or schools"

**Verdict: FALSE on schools; MIXED on hospitals**

**Finding:**
- **Schools:** UNESCO estimates there are roughly 5.5 million primary and secondary schools globally, with additional millions of tertiary, vocational, and informal educational institutions. The World Christian Database estimates roughly 5 million Christian congregations/churches worldwide (including house churches and small gatherings). It is extremely unlikely that churches outnumber all schools globally.
- **Hospitals:** The World Health Organization estimates there are roughly 165,000 hospitals worldwide. Churches (5M+) clearly outnumber hospitals.

The speaker's claim appears to combine "more than hospitals" (true) with "more than schools" (false). The exact wording in the transcript is: "there are more churches in this world than hospitals or schools." The "or" makes the statement false if either category exceeds churches.

**Sources:**
- [UNESCO — Institute for Statistics (schools data)](https://uis.unesco.org/)
- [WHO — Global Health Observatory (hospitals data)](https://www.who.int/data/gho)
- [World Christian Database — congregational estimates](https://worldchristiandatabase.org/)

## Claim 10: "I recently did another important series... about who biblical Israel really is" / "the Israelites are the Negroes scattered around the world"

**Verdict: UNVERIFIABLE (theological/identity claim)**

**Finding:** The speaker's claim that biblical Israel corresponds to the African diaspora is a central tenet of Black Hebrew Israelite theology. This is not a verifiable historical or demographic fact: it is a religious-identity claim rejected by mainstream genetics, historiography, and biblical scholarship. As noted in the master fact-check document, "the question of who counts as 'Israel' today is fundamentally a religious-identity claim and arguments against it are unlikely to be persuasive on factual grounds alone." It is outside the scope of fact-checking.

**Sources:**
- See SUMMARY_AND_FACTCHECK.md scope note on Black Hebrew Israelite identity theology.

## Claim 11: "The word 'Christian' is literally written in your King James Bible" — referring to Acts 11:26 and 1 Peter 4:16

**Verdict: TRUE**

**Finding:** The word "Christian" appears in the King James Bible in Acts 11:26 ("the disciples were called Christians first in Antioch"), Acts 26:28, and 1 Peter 4:16 ("if any man suffer as a Christian"). The speaker's specific citation of Acts 11:26 and 1 Peter 4:16 is accurate. The next episode is explicitly framed around explaining why he rejects the label despite its presence in scripture.

**Sources:**
- [Bible Gateway — Acts 11:26 (KJV)](https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search=Acts%2011%3A26&version=KJV)
- [Bible Gateway — 1 Peter 4:16 (KJV)](https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search=1%20Peter%204%3A16&version=KJV)
