### Profiles of Selected Early Church Fathers (lines 48–97)
- **Eusebius** (lines 48–54):
  - Church father of the 3rd and 4th century.
  - Bishop of Caesarea during Constantine's reign.
  - Formulated church history and made it represent Christianity.
  - His *Ecclesiastical History* is the principal source for church history from the 1st century down to Constantine.
  - Most history of early Christianity comes from Eusebius.
- **Irenaeus** (lines 55–60):
  - Greek bishop from the 2nd century (c. 130–200 AD).
  - Guided and expanded Christian communities in southern Gaul (present-day France).
  - Developed Christian theology by combating Gnostic interpretations.
  - Known as "the Doctrine of Unity."
  - Connected to the idea of the "universal church."
- **Justin Martyr** (lines 61–69):
  - Also known as "Justin the Philosopher."
  - Apologist from the early 2nd century (c. 110–165 AD).
  - Greek philosopher who converted to Christianity.
  - Died as a martyr under Marcus Aurelius.
  - According to Britannica, his writings represent "one of the first positive encounters of Christian revelation with Greek philosophy" and laid the basis for a theology of history.
  - Described as a pagan who converted to Christianity.
- **Lactantius** (lines 73–78):
  - Early Christian author from the mid-3rd to early 4th century.
  - Advisor to Roman Emperor Constantine, guiding his Christian religious policy.
  - Tutor to Constantine's son, Crispus.
  - Helped create Rome as a Christian empire.
- **Origen** (lines 79–91):
  - 3rd-century Christian scholar (c. 185–251 AD).
  - Student of Clement of Alexandria.
  - Major contributor to Christian theology.
  - Widely regarded as one of the most influential Christian theologians; known as "the father of Christian theology."
  - Known for contributions to the development of the Trinity and the ransom theory of atonement.
  - Pioneer of philosophical theology in the church.
  - Speaker claims: "Much of your Christian doctrine comes from this man."
- **Tertullian** (lines 92–97):
  - Early church father of the 2nd and 3rd century.
  - First theologian to write in Latin.
  - Called "the father of Latin Christianity" and "the founder of Western theology."
  - These are "not all the men, they're just some of the men."
