# Episode 1 Summary — Testing Christianity Part 1: Introduction / The Test

**Source:** `transcripts/01 - TESTING_CHRISTIANITY_PT._1_INTRODUCTION_THE_TEST.txt`  
**Approx. length:** ~24 minutes (per series overview)

---

## Opening Scripture & Prophetic Framing (lines 1–11)

- The episode opens with a quotation of **2 Timothy 4:2–4** (KJV-style): "Preach the word. Be ready in season and out of season. Convince, rebuke, exhort with all longsuffering and teaching. For the time will come when they will not endure sound doctrine... they will heap up for themselves teachers... and be turned aside to fables."
- The speaker frames this as a prophecy that is being fulfilled in the present day, specifically with regard to audience reactions to his topic.
- He notes that when he has raised this subject before, people have proven the prophecy true by rejecting uncomfortable truths in favor of teachers who tell them what they want to hear.

## Eschatological Urgency & Personal Stakes (lines 12–21)

- The speaker declares: "It's apparent that we are in the last days. We are in the end times."
- He acknowledges that this realization can evoke either excitement or anxiety depending on one's level of faith.
- Core question posed: "Are you ready?"
- He suggests the biggest risk of unreadiness comes from following the wrong influence.

## The Provocation: Is Christianity Satan-Proof? (lines 18–35)

- The speaker references a prior video reviewing world religions and their beliefs about end times, noting that all other religions lead to the Antichrist.
- He then poses a deliberately provocative question: **Is mainstream Christianity also leading to the Antichrist?**
- Rather than assuming Christianity is exempt from deception because of its size, he asks whether depth of deception might exist within the faith itself.
- He brings up the statistic that Christianity is the largest religion in the world (~one-third of global population) and asks: "Does that mean that it's only the rest of the world that's deceived and not Christians?"
- He proposes that perhaps "some Christians are deceived, but not you, or not your denomination."
- Notes that of ~2.5 billion self-identified Christians, more than half are Catholic, so some may think the deception lies specifically with Catholicism rather than their own practice.

## Speaker's Personal Background (lines 30–62)

- Speaker identifies as having been Christian "a majority of my whole life."
- Background: predominantly Baptist, later non-denominational.
- States personal love for "our Father" and "our Messiah" and belief in salvation "through the blood of Messiah."
- Describes a period years ago when he began to see a "major problem" with Christianity and made excuses to avoid confronting it.
- Explicitly frames the topic as dangerous and hard to hear, but insists it is the truth.
- Assures viewers he is not trying to undermine belief in the Messiah or the Bible itself, nor the "way, the actual faith that the apostles had."
- Anticipates defensive reactions: "oh he's wrong, just another blasphemer" — notes that some long-time subscribers have unsubscribed based on video titles alone without hearing the content.

## The Problem of Unanswered Questions (lines 62–91)

- Recounts growing up in a Christian home (mother and grandmother strong Christians; both sides of family Christian).
- As he got older and started his own family, he began asking questions.
- The same "strong Christians" who raised him got angry when he asked questions, rejected him, and called him prideful.
- Connects this dynamic to why many of his friends reject the Bible and the church: they have questions that go unanswered.
- Calls it hypocrisy: if you are "strong on something, you should be able to answer questions about why."
- Specific questions he asked:
  - Where did the Christmas tree come from?
  - Why do we celebrate Christmas on December 25th?
  - Couldn't find these in the Bible.
- States his intention was not to go against the faith, but to get clarity.
- Realized he had been "wronged" by people avoiding answers and instead getting angry at the questions.

## The Journey to Self-Study & Discovery (lines 82–133)

- This experience, over a decade ago, led him to study Christianity for himself without relying on people he was "offending."
- Led him straight to reading the Bible for himself.
- Found after repeated readings that **"the faith of Christianity does not actually match with the faith that I was actually reading about in the scriptures."**
- Describes the consequences as life-altering: without this understanding, he believes he would be leading an immoral life, thinking too much about money, voting tribally or politically, while falsely believing himself saved.
- Emphasizes this is a description of the Christians he knew in his own life, not a blanket statement about all Christians.
- Notes that the Messiah prophesied multiple times that this problem would arise.

## Internal Struggle & Decision to Speak (lines 134–173)

- Describes his own internal resistance:
  - Fear: "What if I'm wrong? I'm literally damning myself and my family to hell."
  - Scale: "A third of the world are Christians... I'm literally going against the beliefs of a third of the world population."
  - Audience risk: "Is it worth stirring up a pot like this when I have such a large audience?"
- Concluded it is worth stirring up because it's "extremely important."
- Believes Yahuwah gave him an audience for a purpose and must use it for God's will, not to protect himself from conflict.
- Explains the name of his ministry, "Truth Unedited," as reflecting that truth has been edited and these things need to be brought out regardless of backlash.

## Framing the Argument: This Is Not About Labels (lines 229–304)

- Explicitly states: "This is not a fight about a label."
- Tells a story of a sincere brother who kept fighting for the label "Christianity" until he understood the speaker was talking about the religion as a system, not the label.
- Challenges viewers: if they became Christian in total isolation with zero influence, they should share their story in the comments. He says he has never met anyone like this.
- Argues that even reading the Bible alone does not mean one is without influence because Christianity is the biggest religious influence globally, embedded in culture.
- Points out that especially in the United States, Christianity is historically and culturally dominant: "The United States was once classified... as a Christian country."
- Concludes that for anyone in the U.S. to claim they had no influence from Christianity is naive.

## Foundational Doctrinal Error #1: Covenant Misunderstanding (lines 305–327)

- Returns to the question: "Who is our father, Yahuwah, the Most High? Who is he actually in covenant with according to the Bible?"
- States that while it may not be a written doctrine, Christians as a whole believe God is in covenant with "Christians" and that the Bible is ultimately about them.
- Calls this "a very wrong thought to have" with huge implications.
- Claims there is no mainstream challenge to this false understanding in churches.
- Promises to back this up as the series progresses.

## The Five-Question Test (lines 328–391)

- Introduces the central device of the episode: five simple questions that Christians should be able to answer biblically.
- Provides the questions:
  1. **Who did Yahuwah make the new covenant with?**
  2. **What scripture gives the covenant for Christians?**
  3. **What does the Father plan to do in the last days before Messiah comes back and becomes king?**
  4. **What are you waiting for the Father to do with Christians during these last days?** (Pre-tribulation rapture? Bunker/hide during tribulation?)
  5. **Are we trying to go to heaven or stay on earth?**
- Created a PDF of these questions; link provided in description.
- States he will not answer them now but will cover them biblically by the end of the series, at which point viewers can review their own and others' answers.
- Contends that for the largest religion in the world, with Bibles so widely available and more churches than hospitals or schools, these questions should be easily and accurately answered by scripture.

## Why This Matters: Satan's Plan & Messiah's Prophecy (lines 392–434)

- **First reason:** Most people don't understand that Satan has a plan for Christians. Once tribulation starts, those who discover their faith was based on religion rather than truth will not be directed into truth, but into "the doctrines of Lucifer."
- Claims these doctrines are already visible now but most don't see it.
- Expresses hope that even if people don't believe him now, when world conditions change they might remember this series and choose to repent.
- Suggests messages like these may be suppressed in the future, referencing COVID-era suppression of dissent.
- **Second reason (most important):** The Messiah prophesied exactly this problem for the last days and explained what would happen to those attached to it.
- Characterizes Christianity as a religion that "on the surface seems biblical" but whose doctrines are actually about "abusing grace, editing covenants, rejecting the way our Father actually chose to reach humanity."
- Calls it a "religion of terror" and urges viewers to "come to our Father in truth."
