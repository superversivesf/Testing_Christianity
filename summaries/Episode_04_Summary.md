# Episode 4 — Take the Test: Answers & Analysis

## Framing Note

This episode evaluates the answers viewers gave to the five-question “test” issued in Part 1. The speaker argues that mainstream Christianity habitually practices “replacement theology” (the church replaces ethnic Israel) and therefore fails the test on every question. Biblical citations and counter-arguments are presented in the speaker’s preferred Hebrew-transliteration frame (Yahuwah, Yahushua, Yashareel, Yehudah). What follows below is a neutral restatement of the arguments, not an endorsement.

## Detailed Summary

### Opening: Christianity as a Counterfeit (Tares) – lines 1‑46
- **Recap of Part 3 thesis:** Christianity is the “religion of tares” Satan planted alongside the true seed (Matthew 13:24‑43). When fully grown, its fruit is “completely opposite of the scriptures.”
- **Refuting the “original faith” objection:** The speaker argues that leaving Christianity is not leaving the Bible; it is “embracing and enforcing the word.” He claims the powers-that-be have manufactured the impression that Christianity is the original faith, which he calls “very far from the truth.”
- **Montage / cultural critique of modern Christian identity (lines 14‑27):**
  - Cites the ease with which celebrities and ordinary people self-identify as Christian without doctrinal or behavioral alignment.
  - Includes an audio clip (attributed to Kim Kardashian / Kanye West context) in which the speaker is told, “I think we’re more spiritual… I am Christian… my favorite bible verse is acts something.”
  - Uses this to illustrate that “anybody can call themselves a Christian and people believe it.”
- **Kirk Franklin as emblem of the problem (lines 28‑37):**
  - Names gospel artist Kirk Franklin as “the best example of Christianity today.”
  - Claims Franklin has “been wicked from the get-go” but was initially “more milder” because the “veil of Christianity” masked the full growth of the tares.
  - Asserts Franklin introduced “subtle changes in gospel music very subtly over time,” and now, in the “last days,” embodies a “faith of do what thou wilt.”
  - Claims that if Christianity were true, “the body as a whole would tell that man to sit his butt down somewhere.”

### Response to a Viewer’s Testimony & The Wheat/Tares Analogy – lines 38‑99
- **Reads a sincere comment from a self-described “born-again believer” (lines 41‑45):**
  - The commenter cites 1 Corinthians 15, describes deliverance from drugs, sex, and homelessness, and notes he is now married with children and a ministry leader at his church.
  - The commenter asks: “Am I a tare?”
- **Speaker’s diagnostic principle:** Nothing in that testimony allows an observer to “make a diagnosis of his faith.” The fact that many Christians clean up their lives in church “means nothing” for determining whether they are wheat or tares.
- **Personal anecdote (lines 49‑77):**
  - Recalls a brother from over ten years ago who was worldly (drunk, smoking weed, immoral) but later went to church, started a family, and entered ministry.
  - The speaker now calls this man “absolutely… a tare” because he still puts up Christmas trees and aligns with mainstream church doctrine.
  - Stresses that lifestyle improvements and church participation are worldly metrics of faith, not biblical ones.
- **Parable of the Wheat and the Tares (lines 78‑84):**
  - Tares look identical to wheat until fully grown; outward righteousness is therefore unreliable as a spiritual test.
  - Acknowledges that this message may anger viewers who grew up in church and believe they are living for God.
- **Why the test matters (lines 85‑99):**
  - Satan causes defensiveness; the test is meant to offer an objective, scripture-based way to settle the debate rather than endless “going in circles.”
  - The speaker says he intentionally waited a week before providing answers so viewers would engage first.

### Question 1 — Who did Yahuwah make the New Covenant with? – lines 114‑183
- **The question restated (line 114):** “Who did our father, Yahuwah, the Most High, make the new covenant with?”
- **Sample answers from commenters (lines 129‑141):**
  - “The new covenant Jesus gave to his 12 disciples.”
  - A covenant with “mankind that he will forgive sin and restore communion with those whose heart believe in his son, Jesus Christ.”
  - Essentially, “Yahuwah made the new covenant with everyone.”
- **Speaker’s critique:** These answers reveal replacement theology — the assumption that the covenant is with Christians or all humanity, rather than with the specific parties named in scripture.
- **Biblical answer given (lines 149‑162):**
  - Reads Jeremiah 31:31‑34, identifying the covenant as made with “the house of Yashareel and with the house of Yehudah” (the houses of Israel and Judah).
  - Notes this passage is quoted in Hebrews 8:8‑11.
- **Clarification on Gentile inclusion (lines 178‑183):**
  - The answer does not reject Gentile salvation; Gentiles are “grafted in” as wild branches.
  - But the speaker insists one cannot “make our own doctrine and ignore what Yah did.”

### Question 2 — What scripture gives the covenant for Christians? – lines 184‑238
- **The question restated (line 184):** “What scripture gives the covenant for Christians?”
- **Sample answers from commenters (lines 186‑197):**
  - One cites Jeremiah 31:31‑34 (the same text already identified as addressed to Israel/Judah).
  - Others cite Luke 22:19‑20 / Matthew 26:26‑29, or a bundle of texts (Jeremiah 31, Romans 9, Romans 11, Galatians 3:28‑29).
  - One commenter says there is no scripture for Christians, but then adds Paul was “raised up to give it to the Gentiles” and cites Galatians 3:28‑29.
- **Speaker’s critique:**
  - People take Jeremiah 31 — which names Israel and Judah — and wrongly apply it to Christians.
  - Misuse of Galatians 3:28‑29 is specifically called out as an example of replacement theology (lines 223‑238).
- **Biblical answer given (lines 206‑209):**
  - “There is no scripture that makes a covenant with Christians. It’s really just as simple as that.”
- **Interpretive claim about Paul and the Maccabean Wars (lines 229‑235):**
  - The speaker claims that when Paul refers to “Greeks” he does not mean all Greeks, but specifically “Yahudim whom assimilated into the Greek culture voluntarily.”
  - States this happened “during the Maccabean Wars” and that understanding that history is essential for reading Paul correctly.

### Question 3 — What does the Father plan to do in the last days before Messiah comes back and becomes king? – lines 239‑276
- **The question restated (lines 239‑240):** “What does our father plan to do in the last days before the Messiah comes back and becomes king?”
- **Sample answers from commenters (lines 242‑248):**
  - “Call out some of the world to follow his son in spirit and truth.”
  - “The father will destroy everything and everybody that is not like him.”
  - “Judgment,” “the great tribulation,” “apostasy,” “appearing of the lawless one,” “two witnesses,” “heavenly signs,” etc.
- **Speaker’s critique:**
  - Christians only think about judgment and Revelation; they “only see judgment because Christianity is preparing you for judgment.”
  - Christianity leaves out the true promises and focuses on fear rather than hope.
- **Biblical answer given (lines 260‑268):**
  - The Father plans to gather Yashareel and Yehudah from the four corners of the earth.
  - Before bringing them into the land, he will bring them into the wilderness to deal with them face to face and purge out rebels.
  - Speaker calls this “the judgment of God” (line 264) and later in the recap labels it the “second exodus.”
  - Cites Jeremiah 23:3‑8 and Isaiah 43:1‑6 as good starting points.

### Question 4 — What are you waiting for the Father to do with Christians during these last days? – lines 277‑319
- **The question restated (lines 278):** “What are you waiting for our father to do with Christians during these last days?”
- **Sample answers from commenters (lines 280‑288):**
  - “Rapture, as in the book of Thessalonians.”
  - “Christians will be kept preserved as they are sealed. Revelation chapter 7 through 9.”
  - “Repent and believe the true Messiah.”
  - “Give the Christians a spirit of repentance and obedience.”
  - “Judgment in the house of God… then the rapture… then wrath of God.”
  - “For Christians it’s either the rapture or revival.”
- **Speaker’s critique:**
  - Expecting the rapture is a by-product of replacement theology; Christians believe they will be “taken off the earth so that they don’t have to go through the problems that the world is going to go through,” while Israel suffers.
  - Some acknowledge “revival on the dry land” (Israel), but because they don’t recognize Yashareel, they miss that this is happening now.
- **Biblical answer given (lines 306‑313):**
  - You should be waiting to be part of the gathering of Yashareel and Yehudah.
  - Wild branches (grafted-in Gentiles) come with them.
  - Those not ready will be tested.
  - Christianity, the speaker argues, does not prepare people for this promise because it does not even teach it.

### Question 5 — Are we trying to go to heaven or stay on earth? – lines 320‑346
- **The question restated (lines 320):** “Are we trying to go to heaven or stay on earth?”
- **Sample answers from commenters (lines 321‑326):**
  - “It depends if you’re a part of the first resurrection or the second… Revelation 21 and 22.”
  - “Heaven.”
  - “We will spend eternity as described in Revelation, New Jerusalem.”
- **Speaker’s critique:**
  - Answers completely skip over the millennial reign on earth and jump straight to “new heaven and new earth” or simply say “heaven.”
  - This reveals an “unbiblical” expectation shaped by Christianity.
- **Biblical answer given (lines 335‑345):**
  - “Our promise is to reign with Messiah on earth.”
  - Cites Zechariah 14 as prophesying Messiah reigning as king on earth over Yashareel and Yehudah, restored as one kingdom (Ezekiel 37).
  - After this thousand-year rule, then Judgment Day, then a new heaven and new earth.
  - Christianity ignores this because it prepares the masses for the beast, not for the kingdom on earth.

### Side-by-Side Recap of Questions – lines 347‑408
- **The speaker repeats all five questions with paired columns:**
  1. *Q1:* Christians say the covenant is with “Christians” (replacement theology). The Bible says it is with Yashareel / Yehudah (Jer 31:31‑34 / Heb 8:8‑11).
  2. *Q2:* Christians cite Jeremiah 31 or mixed scriptures with replacement theology. The Bible says there is no covenant text addressed to “Christians.”
  3. *Q3:* Christians speak only of judgment/Revelation. The Bible says gather scattered Israel/Judah, wilderness purge (Second Exodus; Jer 23:3‑8; Isa 43:1‑6).
  4. *Q4:* Christians speak of rapture or revival. The Bible says wait for Yashareel/Yehudah gathering with grafted-in Gentiles.
  5. *Q5:* Christians say heaven / new heaven & earth. The Bible says reign with Messiah on earth for a thousand years (Zech 14; Ezek 37) before the final state.
- **Challenge to opponents:** The speaker invites pastors and Christians to answer the questions “directly with scripture and not the twisting and bending of scripture.” He claims anyone ignoring the thousand-year earthly reign is “ignoring Yah’s kingdom and his plan.”

### The “One Humble Comment” – lines 409‑446
- **The speaker reads a long comment from a viewer (channel name “Truth Unedited”) as an example of humility (lines 420‑436):**
  - “Wow. If what you’re saying is true, then I am not ready. I will investigate for myself.”
  - The commenter mentions stepping away from pre-tribulation rapture belief, noting “some leaven” in his beliefs.
  - He says he was taught that “natural Israel” would go through the great tribulation and that “this is exclusively the natural branch” (Jacob’s trouble).
  - Acknowledges he must look into “the ones you call fake who are in Israel right now.”
  - Describes trying to teach Matthew verse-by-verse in church but being stopped because “we need to focus on the needs of the people.”
  - Mentions changing diet, stopping man-made festivals, and trying to observe the appointed times for the first time.
- **Speaker’s reaction:**
  - Praises this as “an answer and comment of humility.”
  - Contrasts it with prideful defensiveness; says this attitude will “serve him well.”
  - Restates that the goal is to get both natural and wild branches ready, not to boast.

### Transition to History (Part 5 Preview) – lines 447‑470
- **Announces Part 5 will examine the history of Christianity:** how the label “Christian” came about, and why it is “not by accident and not without influence.”
- **Final characterization of Christianity:**
  - A religion that seems biblical on the surface but, doctrinally, “abuses grace, edits covenants, rejects the way our father actually chose to reach humanity, and makes it only be about the way Christians want to receive him.”
  - “It lacks the love and reverence for our father that he deserves and in the end it does not prepare those who follow it to be ready.”
- **Call to action:** Come out of the religion of tares and “come to our father in truth.”
